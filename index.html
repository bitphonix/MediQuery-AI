About MediQuery

Addressing a critical global healthcare challenge through AI innovation

MediQuery is an advanced medical imaging analysis system designed to provide AI-assisted interpretation of chest X-rays. Using a novel retrieval augmented generation approach, the system combines computer vision and natural language processing to deliver accurate, interpretable analyses.

Developed by Tanishk Soni, MediQuery aims to address the critical shortage of radiological expertise in many parts of the world, where approximately 4 billion people lack access to basic radiological services.

Key Features

Powerful capabilities that make MediQuery an exceptional tool

Attention Mapping

Visualize areas of interest on chest X-rays with advanced attention mapping technology, helping users understand where the system is focusing.

Knowledge Retrieval

Access relevant medical knowledge by retrieving similar cases from a database of 30.6K annotated chest X-rays from the MIMIC-CXR dataset.

Natural Language Analysis

Receive clear, structured radiological assessments formatted in standard medical reporting style with findings and impression sections.

Medical Q&A

Ask specific questions about radiological findings, conditions, and differentials to receive evidence-based explanations.

Anatomical Region Detection

Automatically identifies and labels key anatomical regions in chest X-rays to provide structured analysis of each area.

Confidence Scoring

Transparent confidence metrics help users understand the reliability of the system's analysis for each case.

Technology Stack

Powered by cutting-edge AI technologies

MediQuery utilizes a modified DenseNet121 architecture (CheXNet) for chest X-ray feature extraction. The model has been trained on over 100,000 radiographs and outputs 1024-dimensional embedding vectors representing key image features.

BioBERT, a biomedical language representation model pre-trained on PubMed abstracts and PMC full-text articles, processes natural language queries and generates 768-dimensional embedding vectors of medical concepts.

The FAISS vector database enables efficient similarity search across 30.6K embeddings from the MIMIC-CXR dataset. Hybrid retrieval combines text and image similarity scores with adaptive weighting based on query characteristics.

A fine-tuned FLAN-T5 model generates structured radiological assessments based on retrieved context. The model has been specifically optimized for medical report generation with specialized training on radiologist-authored reports.

Global Impact

Addressing a critical healthcare need worldwide

Global Radiology Gap

Bridging the critical shortage of radiological expertise

According to the World Health Organization, approximately 4 billion people worldwide lack access to basic radiological services. In low-income countries, the ratio of radiologists to population can be as low as 1:1,000,000, compared to 1:10,000 in high-income countries.

Growing demand for radiology services highlights the need for AI-assisted solutions
